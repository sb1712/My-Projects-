# Incident Management – ITIL v4 (Municipal Government)

## Purpose
Restore normal IT services as quickly as possible and minimize business impact to city operations, emergency services, and public-facing systems.

## Scope
Applies to all production systems including:
- Network & Internet
- Microsoft 365 / Email
- ERP / Finance
- HR Systems
- GIS & Public Safety Applications
- Cloud & Data Center Infrastructure

## Incident Priority Matrix

| Priority | Impact | Example | SLA |
|----------|--------|---------|-----|
| P1 – Critical | City-wide outage | Email down, Network failure, 911 systems | 15 min response, 4 hr resolution |
| P2 – High | Department outage | Finance system unavailable | 30 min response, 8 hr resolution |
| P3 – Medium | Single user | Laptop failure | 4 hr response, 2 days |
| P4 – Low | How-to / request | Software install | 1 day response, 5 days |

## Incident Lifecycle
1. Detection (User, Monitoring, SOC, NOC)
2. Logging (ServiceNow / Freshservice)
3. Categorization & Prioritization
4. Initial Diagnosis (L1)
5. Functional Escalation (L2 / L3)
6. Resolution & Recovery
7. Closure & User Confirmation
8. Post-Incident Review (for P1/P2)

## Escalation Model
- L1: Service Desk
- L2: Systems / Network / Application Support
- L3: Cloud / Security / Vendor

## Major Incident Process
- Bridge Call activation
- War room coordination
- Executive communication
- Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
- Preventive action plan

## KPIs
- Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR)
- First Call Resolution (FCR)
- SLA Compliance %
- Reopen Rate
- Incident Backlog Aging
